Instead of one train/test split, rotate through 5 different splits. Gives more reliable performance estimates, especially with small clinical datasets.

SHAP: The Intuition

"Imagine a team of doctors making a diagnosis by committee. SHAP asks: if we removed each doctor one at a time, how much would the diagnosis change? That change is each doctor's contribution — SHAP does the same for model features."

Section 3 — Explainability & Ethics

Bias & Fairness in Mental Health AI

⚠️ Specific Risks
  • Training data biased toward white, male, high-SES patients
  • ASD "female phenotype" systematically underrepresented — later diagnosis, different presentation
  • Indigenous and racialized youth underrepresented in most datasets
  • Socioeconomic confounders (access to care → diagnosis rates)
  • Historical inequities encoded in training labels
✅ Responsible AI Checklist
  • Who is in your training data? Are all groups represented?
  • Does model performance differ by race, sex, or SES?
  • Have community members been involved in design?
  • Is there a post-deployment audit mechanism?
  • Is a human always in the loop for clinical decisions?
📄 Ref: Obermeyer et al. (2019). Dissecting racial bias in an algorithm used to manage the health of populations. Science, 366(6464), 447–453.
This often generates strong reactions — be prepared for extended discussion. The Obermeyer paper: "A widely deployed clinical algorithm systematically underestimated the health needs of Black patients because it used healthcare cost as a proxy for health need — encoding historical disparities in access to care." Similar risks exist in every mental health dataset built on diagnosed cases.

Section 5 — Clinical Integration

A Framework for Responsible Clinical AI

🔍
Transparency
Can you explain how the model works? Would you be comfortable explaining its decision to a patient?
⚖️
Fairness
Does the model perform equally across demographic groups? Who was excluded from training data?
🔐
Privacy
Is patient data protected? Are you compliant with PIPEDA/PHIPA? Is your data governance documented?
✅
Clinical Validity
Has the model been prospectively validated in your clinical population — not just the research dataset?
👤
Accountability
Who is responsible when the model is wrong? Is there always a human in the loop for clinical decisions?
The Human-in-the-Loop requirement is non-negotiable for clinical decision support.
Ref: Montreal Declaration for Responsible AI Development (2018)
"Print this slide. Put it in your grant applications and ethics submissions. Funding agencies are increasingly requiring this kind of framework explicitly. The five pillars map directly to what REBs and health technology assessment bodies are asking for." The human-in-the-loop point is non-negotiable — it's both an ethical requirement and a regulatory expectation.
